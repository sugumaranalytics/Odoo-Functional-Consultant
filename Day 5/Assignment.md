# Assignment – Day 5

## Scenario

You are an **Odoo Functional Consultant**.

A new company named **ABC Technologies** has started using Odoo to manage its customers and vendors.

The company has:

- Customers
- Vendors
- Contact Persons

Your responsibility is to configure the Contacts module and train employees on managing business contacts.

Answer the following questions.

---

# 1. What is the Contacts Module in Odoo?

**Answer:**

The Contacts module is the central repository for managing all business contacts in Odoo.

It stores information about:

- Customers
- Vendors
- Companies
- Individual Contacts

The same contact information is shared across Sales, Purchase, CRM, Inventory, Accounting, and other modules.

---

# 2. How do you create a new customer?

**Answer:**

Steps:

1. Open the **Contacts** module.
2. Click **New**.
3. Select **Company** or **Individual**.
4. Enter:
   - Name
   - Address
   - Phone
   - Mobile
   - Email
   - Website
5. Click **Save**.

The customer is now available for use in Sales, CRM, and Accounting.

---

# 3. How do you create a vendor?

**Answer:**

Steps:

1. Open the **Contacts** module.
2. Click **New**.
3. Select **Company**.
4. Enter vendor details:
   - Company Name
   - Address
   - Phone
   - Email
   - Website
5. Click **Save**.

The vendor can now be used in the Purchase module.

---

# 4. Why do we add Contact Persons?

**Answer:**

A company may have multiple employees responsible for different activities.

Examples:

- Purchase Manager
- Sales Manager
- Accounts Executive
- HR Manager

Adding contact persons helps maintain accurate communication with the correct employee.

---

# 5. What is the difference between a Company and an Individual Contact?

**Answer:**

| Company | Individual Contact |
|----------|--------------------|
| Represents an organization | Represents a person |
| Can have multiple contact persons | Belongs to a company or exists independently |
| Used for customers and vendors | Used for employees or company representatives |

Example:

**Company:** XYZ Suppliers

**Contact Person:** Raj Kumar – Purchase Manager

---

# 6. Why is the Contacts module important?

**Answer:**

The Contacts module is used by multiple Odoo applications.

Examples:

- Sales
- Purchase
- CRM
- Inventory
- Accounting
- Helpdesk

Updating contact information once automatically updates it wherever the contact is used.

---

# 7. What information can be stored in a contact?

**Answer:**

A contact record can store:

- Company Name
- Contact Person
- Address
- City
- State
- Country
- Phone Number
- Mobile Number
- Email Address
- Website
- GSTIN / Tax ID
- Tags
- Internal Notes

This information supports daily business operations.

---

# 8. What is the purpose of the Chatter?

**Answer:**

The Chatter records all activities related to a contact.

Examples:

- Contact Created
- Address Updated
- Phone Number Changed
- Email Updated
- Notes
- Scheduled Activities

It provides a complete history of changes and communication.

---

# 9. How does the Contacts module integrate with other modules?

**Answer:**

The Contacts module shares contact information with:

- CRM
- Sales
- Purchase
- Inventory
- Accounting
- Helpdesk

Example:

A customer created in the Contacts module can immediately be selected while creating a Sales Quotation.

Similarly, a vendor created in Contacts can be selected while creating a Purchase Request for Quotation (RFQ).

---

# 10. What are the responsibilities of an Odoo Functional Consultant?

**Answer:**

An Odoo Functional Consultant should:

- Create customer and vendor records.
- Add contact persons.
- Maintain accurate contact information.
- Configure contact details.
- Train users to manage contacts.
- Explain how contacts are shared across modules.
- Ensure business data remains accurate and up to date.

---

# Conclusion

The **Contacts Module** is the foundation of customer and vendor management in Odoo.

As an **Odoo Functional Consultant**, understanding how to create and maintain contacts is essential because the same information is used throughout Sales, Purchase, CRM, Inventory, and Accounting.

Proper contact management improves communication, reduces duplicate data, and supports efficient business operations.
