# What is CRM?

CRM stands for

**Customer Relationship Management**

CRM is used to manage customer interactions from the first inquiry until the customer purchases a product or service.

It helps businesses:

- Store customer information
- Track sales opportunities
- Schedule follow-ups
- Increase sales
- Improve customer relationships

---

# Why CRM is Important

Without CRM

- Customer details may be lost
- Follow-ups may be forgotten
- Sales opportunities may be missed

With CRM

- Everything is organized
- Sales team can track customers
- Better customer satisfaction
- Higher sales conversion

# Lead

A Lead is a potential customer.

Example

Someone visits your website and fills a contact form.

Customer may or may not buy.

---

# Opportunity

An Opportunity is a qualified lead.

The customer has genuine interest.

Example

ABC Technologies wants ERP implementation.

Budget

₹50,000

Now it becomes an Opportunity.

---

# CRM Pipeline

The Pipeline shows the current sales status.

Example

```
New

↓

Qualified

↓

Proposition

↓

Won
```

Every Opportunity moves through these stages.

---

# Pipeline Stages

## New

Customer inquiry received.

Example

Customer called asking for information.

---

## Qualified

Customer is interested.

Requirements are understood.

Budget is available.

---

## Proposition

Quotation has been prepared or sent.

Customer is reviewing the offer.

---

## Won

Customer accepted the quotation.

Sales Order will be created.

---

## Lost

Customer rejected the quotation.

Reasons may include

- High price
- Competitor selected
- Budget issues
