# Objective

Understand the CRM module, create opportunities, manage the sales pipeline, schedule activities, and prepare opportunities for the sales process.

---



## Q1. What is CRM?

### Answer

CRM (Customer Relationship Management) is a system used to manage customer information, leads, opportunities, follow-ups, quotations, and customer relationships from the first inquiry until the sale is completed.

---

## Q2. What does CRM stand for?

### Answer

**Customer Relationship Management**

---

## Q3. Write any four benefits of CRM.

### Answer

1. Better customer relationship management.
2. Easy tracking of leads and opportunities.
3. Improves follow-up activities.
4. Increases sales and customer satisfaction.

---

## Q4. What is the difference between a Lead and an Opportunity?

| Lead | Opportunity |
|------|-------------|
| Potential customer | Qualified customer |
| Customer may or may not buy | Customer is interested in buying |
| Initial stage | Sales stage |
| Less information available | Requirements and budget are known |

---

## Q5. What is the purpose of the CRM Pipeline?

### Answer

The CRM Pipeline helps track opportunities through different sales stages such as New, Qualified, Proposition, Won, and Lost.

---

## Q6. Explain the following stages.

### **New**

A new customer inquiry has been received.

### **Qualified**

The customer is interested and meets the business requirements.

### **Proposition**

A quotation or proposal has been sent to the customer.

### **Won**

The customer accepted the quotation and the sale is successful.

### **Lost**

The customer rejected the quotation or purchased from another company.

---

## Q7. What is Expected Revenue?

### Answer

Expected Revenue is the estimated amount that may be earned from an opportunity.

**Example:** ₹50,000

---

## Q8. Why are Activities used?

### Answer

Activities are used to schedule follow-ups like calls, meetings, emails, and reminders.

---

## Q9. What are Internal Notes?

### Answer

Internal Notes are private notes visible only to company employees.

**Example:**

> Customer requested a demo next week.

---

## Q10. What are Tags used for?

### Answer

Tags help categorize opportunities for easy searching, filtering, and reporting.

**Examples**

- ERP
- Odoo
- Manufacturing
- Retail
- Chennai

**Answer**
